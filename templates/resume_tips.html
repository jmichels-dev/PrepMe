Resume Tips

Many applicants submit resumes to highlight their work experience, accolades, and other out of school involvements. Below is an example resume with some helpful tips.
  • Pick a template that is both modest but also somewhat unique; you want your resume to grab the reviewer's attention but not to be too ostentatious
  • Make sure the resume fits nicely on a single page. Focus on the things that you devoted the most of your time to
  • Don't put anything on your resume that happened before high school, unless it was something that started before and continued throughout, like Boy Scouts
  • Provide as much detail as possible about the information on your resume
  • Use your resume as a tool to highlight things about yourself that you may not have covered as much as you would like in your application
  • Read your resume out loud to enusre everything sounds correct, and ensure there are no spelling or grammatical errors
  • Be honest about what you put in your resume. Admissions officers can contact your school officials to verify information about you
